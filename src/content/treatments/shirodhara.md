## What is Shirodhara?

Shirodhara is one of the most well-known Ayurvedic therapies. The name comes from two Sanskrit words — "Shiro" (head) and "Dhara" (stream). In this treatment, a continuous stream of warm medicated oil, herbal decoction, or medicated buttermilk is gently poured on the forehead, specifically on the "third eye" area (Ajna Marma point).

## How It Works

The rhythmic pouring of warm liquid on the forehead produces a deeply calming effect on the nervous system. It stimulates the pineal gland, regulates serotonin and melatonin levels, and induces a state of deep relaxation similar to meditation. The specific medicated oil used is selected based on the patient's condition.

## Benefits

- Profoundly calms the mind and relieves mental stress
- Reduces anxiety, depression, and insomnia
- Helps manage headaches and migraines
- Lowers blood pressure naturally
- Improves concentration and mental clarity
- Nourishes hair and scalp
- Balances Vata and Pitta doshas

## Conditions It Helps

- Stress, anxiety, and depression
- Insomnia and sleep disorders
- Chronic headaches and migraines
- Hypertension
- Memory loss and lack of concentration
- Premature greying of hair

## What to Expect

You will lie comfortably on a treatment table. A specially designed pot (Dhara Patra) is hung above your forehead. Warm medicated oil flows in a thin, steady stream onto your forehead for 45–60 minutes. Many patients describe the experience as deeply meditative. Rest is recommended after the session.
