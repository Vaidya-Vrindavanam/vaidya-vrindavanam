## What is Katee Vasthi & Greeva Vasthi?

Katee Vasthi and Greeva Vasthi are localised Ayurvedic treatments where warm medicated oil is retained in a reservoir built on the body using a dough dam. Katee Vasthi targets the lower back (lumbo-sacral region), while Greeva Vasthi targets the neck and upper back (cervical region). These are among the most effective treatments for spinal conditions.

## How It Works

A circular dam is built on the affected area using a ring of black gram dough (Urad dal). Warm medicated oil — selected based on the specific condition — is poured into this reservoir and allowed to pool over the area for a sustained period. The oil is kept warm by periodically replacing cooled oil with fresh warm oil. The deep, prolonged oleation nourishes the spinal structures, lubricates vertebral joints, and relaxes the surrounding muscles.

## Benefits

- Provides deep relief from back and neck pain
- Lubricates and nourishes spinal discs
- Relaxes spinal muscles and reduces spasms
- Reduces nerve compression symptoms
- Improves flexibility and range of motion
- Strengthens the musculoskeletal structures
- Prevents recurrence of spinal conditions

## Conditions It Helps

- Lumbar spondylosis and disc problems (Katee Vasthi)
- Cervical spondylosis (Greeva Vasthi)
- Sciatica and referred leg pain
- Chronic lower back pain
- Neck pain and stiffness
- Disc herniation and bulging
- Post-surgical back and neck rehabilitation

## What to Expect

The session lasts 30–45 minutes. You will lie face down (for Katee Vasthi) or face up (for Greeva Vasthi). The therapist builds a dough dam on your lower back or neck area and fills it with warm medicated oil. The oil is maintained at a comfortable warm temperature throughout the session. After the oil is removed, a gentle massage of the area follows. Most patients experience significant relief even after the first session. A course of 7–14 sessions is recommended for chronic conditions.
