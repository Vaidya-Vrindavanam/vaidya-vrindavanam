## Ayurvedic Approach to Varicose Veins

In Ayurveda, varicose veins are understood as Siraja Granthi — a condition where Vata dosha vitiates the blood vessels (Sira), causing them to become dilated, tortuous, and painful. Contributing factors include prolonged standing, Vata-aggravating lifestyle, weakened blood vessel walls, and impaired blood circulation.

## How We Treat Varicose Veins

At Vaidya Vrindavanam, our approach includes:

1. **Blood purification** — Raktamoksham to remove stagnant, impure blood from the affected veins
2. **Thermal therapy** — Agnikarmam at specific points to improve local circulation and reduce venous congestion
3. **Circulation support** — Abhyangam with Vata-pacifying oils to improve venous return and reduce heaviness
4. **Internal medicines** — Herbal formulations to strengthen blood vessel walls and improve blood circulation
5. **Lifestyle guidance** — Leg elevation techniques, compression advice, and exercise recommendations

## Recommended Treatments

- **Raktamoksham** — Therapeutic bloodletting to relieve venous congestion
- **Agnikarmam** — Thermal cauterization at specific points for circulation improvement
- **Abhyangam & Swedam** — Oil massage with gentle steam for improved venous return

## Duration

A treatment course of 7–14 days is recommended, with follow-up sessions as needed. Early-stage varicose veins respond well to Ayurvedic management and can prevent progression to more severe stages.
