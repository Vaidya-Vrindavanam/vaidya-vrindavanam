## Ayurvedic Approach to Asthma

In Ayurveda, asthma is known as Tamaka Shwasa — a condition caused by the vitiation of Kapha and Vata doshas in the respiratory channels (Pranavaha Srotas). Excess Kapha produces mucus that blocks the airways, while aggravated Vata causes spasms in the bronchial passages, leading to breathlessness, wheezing, and coughing.

## How We Treat Respiratory Conditions

At Vaidya Vrindavanam, our approach includes:

1. **Kapha elimination** — Vamanam (therapeutic emesis) to remove excess mucus and Kapha from the respiratory system
2. **Nasal therapy** — Nasyam to clear the nasal passages and upper airways
3. **External therapies** — Chest-focused Abhyangam and steam therapy to loosen congestion
4. **Internal medicines** — Herbal formulations to strengthen lung function and reduce inflammation
5. **Diet & lifestyle** — Kapha-pacifying diet, breathing exercises, and avoidance of triggers

## Recommended Treatments

- **Nasyam** — Nasal administration of medicated oils to clear airways
- **Vamanam** — Therapeutic emesis to eliminate Kapha and mucus
- **Abhyangam & Swedam** — Oil massage with steam therapy focused on the chest

## Duration

A treatment course of 7–14 days is recommended for acute episodes. For chronic asthma and allergic conditions, a longer course of 14–21 days with follow-up sessions yields better results.
